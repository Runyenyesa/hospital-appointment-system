1. ABSTRACT

The Hospital Appointment and Medical Records Management System (HAM-RMS) is a comprehensive web-based application designed to bridge the operational gaps between administrative scheduling and clinical documentation. Traditional systems often fail to facilitate effective communication when scheduling conflicts arise, leading to high cancellation rates and patient dissatisfaction. HAM-RMS introduces an innovative Negotiation Engine that allows staff and patients to propose alternative times within the same request lifecycle. Developed using PHP, MySQL, and Bootstrap 5, the system ensures data integrity, security, and scalability. The project outcome is a functional, user-centric system that significantly reduces administrative overhead and improves the patient-doctor experience through interactive scheduling.

2. CHAPTER ONE: INTRODUCTION

2.1 Background of the Study

The healthcare industry is increasingly reliant on Information and Communication Technology (ICT) to manage the massive influx of patient data and complex scheduling needs. Efficient appointment management is critical to the survival of modern clinics, as it directly impacts patient throughput and resource utilization. In the current landscape, many hospitals still rely on disparate systems for booking and medical records, leading to "data silos."

2.2 Motivation

The primary motivation for this project is the observation of "lost appointments"—instances where a patient is rejected due to a conflict and never returns to the facility. By creating a system that treats an appointment as a collaborative agreement rather than a binary transaction, we can significantly improve the conversion of requests into visits.

3. CHAPTER TWO: PROBLEM STATEMENT

Existing hospital management systems often operate as static databases rather than interactive tools. Key issues identified include:

  • Inflexible Rescheduling: When a doctor is unavailable, the appointment is usually cancelled, forcing a manual follow-up.
  • Data Fragmentation: Completed appointments often do not show up in history if no medical record (diagnosis/prescription) was created.
  • Information Asymmetry: Patients are often unaware of why an appointment was rejected or rescheduled.

4. CHAPTER THREE: METHODOLOGY

The Iterative Agile Model was selected for this project. Unlike the traditional Waterfall model, Agile allowed us to develop the system in "Sprints." This ensured that any architectural flaws were caught early and that features like the negotiation engine could be refined based on testing.
